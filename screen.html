Me of the Screen

Brief: Design an infographic about our own embodied interactions with the digital tools and environments that populate our daily lives.

Observe myself during the interaction when I play Ring Fit Adventure, compare data report from my Apple watch.

FINAL OUTCOME

Assumption

  • gameplay>fitness quality?
  • Accuracy of data?
  • Safety for even beginner?
  • Can it imporve my health/work out?
  • Why I give up to use it before?

Research

Quantitative Information

Observe me during this interaction, spread out for four days. Take notes, screenshots for the game, track the data and reorganize the number.

Qualitative Information

  • if my exercise pose is not acquire, the game remind me
  • the game system doesn't let me repeat the same pose in a short time, not over fatigue. e.g. Squat, not lower enough, it remind me on the screen not hold enough time, can fail
  • doing exercise wise for the game, because different pose has different function
  • There are some "meaningful choices". For example, I can choose different actions to attack monsters by myself. In order to attract the monsters as soon as possible, I may choose the exercise with higher scores. Although this action may be more tiring, it is also possible that this action will be selected the most in a certain mission.
  • Bow Pull and Knee Lift exercise more times, because they have higher damage value, and I will be more inclined to choose them to attack monsters.

how you felt?

In general, it's a gamification model, this kind of sense makes me involuntarily look forward to what better ideas the next level will have. Compared with simply doing aerobics on YouTube, the unknown of game design will drive my curiosity. I love the feedback about when I wear the equipment, through the combination with real sports, the game interface design is easier to understand and operate. I wonder if these advantages can be the motivation for me to keep using them. Secondly, I don't think I sweat as much and quickly as I do in the gym, or maybe I haven't used it long enough or some actions are actually lazy. What's interesting is that I do feel tired after workout out for about 30 minutes. In addition to the processing of the action design, the visual of the scene is also very beautiful. There are seasons changing when I run in the scene, and all the way is pleasing to the eye, and the feeling of boredom in the exercise itself will be reduced. However, the tasks interspersed from time to time, such as collecting gold coins and experience values, fighting monsters and getting treasure chests. These make me more excited than in the gym, driven by progress and a sense of achievement, and the exercise time will be reached unconsciously. But I wonder if I'm more eager to clear the missions, or do I have to do every action more carefully? Instead of giving out the whole exercise skills library for players to choose by themselves, it makes some algorithm rules, and when the skill points reach the designed value, or when some experience values are upgraded, it randomly gives a new action when opening the treasure chest. My current level can only be equipped with six skills. I wonder this design will be more effective for the purpose of exercise?

what you thought about?

Since the Ring fit came out, I have been skeptical. I wonder if the combination of fitness and games is just a marketing gimmick? Although I admit that somatosensory game combination has always been Nintendo's specialty. But at the same time, I'm also thinking about whether the innovative design of adding interactive experience to exercises will make fitness more interesting.

why you were engaging this technology in the first place?

In the beginning, I was attracted by the advertisement, which happened to be the time of lockdown. At first, I was enthusiastic about using it, but the heat soon dropped, and it has been dusty for almost two years. I have been busy with my study and work schedule recently, so I spent less and less time in the gym room. Then I started to use Ring Fit again to see if it would be an alternative option.
